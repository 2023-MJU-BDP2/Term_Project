▲ 인천 만석 우회고가교 철거 후 공사 완료 된 모습(사진제공:인천시) (C) 박상도 기자

(브레이크뉴스인천 박상도 기자) 인천시가 22일, 30여 년의 세월을 지나온 만석 우회고가교가 철거되고 왕복 6차로의 평면도로로 탈바꿈했다고 밝혔다.

인천시에 따르면, 중구 항동1가와 동구 만석동을 연결하는 만석 우회고가교 정비사업이 준공됐으며 이 사업은 지난 2022년 1월 착공해, 총사업비 493억(국비 45억) 원이 투입됐다.

인천중부경찰서 뒤편에서 만석동 주공아파트 구간을 잇는 만석 우회고가교는 1993년에 길이 1.2㎞, 높이 15m 규모로 설치돼 산업도로의 역할을 해 왔지만, 도시미관 훼손과 도시기능 단절로 원도심 활성화의 걸림돌로 작용했다.

이에 따라 인천시는 원도심 활성화를 위한 인천개항창조도시 재생사업의 마중물 사업으로 우회고가교(L=1.27km)를 철거하고 평면교차로 왕복 6차로의 도로(L=1.6km)로 정비하는 사업을 추진해 왔다.

또, 인천시는 코레일 및 국가철도공단과 긴밀히 협의해 우회고가교와 철도 건널목(궤도)을 함께 철거해, 도로선형을 개선하고 교통안전사고도 예방할 수 있는 보행자 중심의 도로를 구축할 수 있었다.

Incheon Manseok Bypass Overpass transformed into a 6-lane flat road.

Removal of railroad crossings and construction of pedestrian-oriented roads

-Break News Incheon Reporter Park Sang-do

Incheon City announced on the 22nd that the Manseok bypass bridge, which had been around for over 30 years, was demolished and transformed into a flat road with six lanes in both directions.

According to Incheon City, the Manseok Bypass Viaduct maintenance project connecting Hangdong 1-ga, Jung-gu and Manseok-dong, Dong-gu has been completed. Construction began in January 2022, and a total project cost of 49.3 billion won (4.5 billion national budget) was invested.

The Manseok Bypass Viaduct, which connects the section behind the Incheon Central Police Station to the Jugong Apartments in Manseok-dong, was installed in 1993 with a length of 1.2km and a height of 15m and has served as an industrial road. However, it has acted as an obstacle to revitalizing the original city center by damaging the urban aesthetic and cutting off urban functions. did.

Accordingly, Incheon City has been promoting a project to demolish the bypass viaduct (L=1.27km) and reorganize it into a 6-lane road (L=1.6km) with a level intersection as a priming project for the Incheon Open Port Creative City regeneration project to revitalize the original downtown.

In addition, Incheon City, in close consultation with KORAIL and the National Railroad Corporation, was able to demolish the bypass overpass and railroad crossing (track) together to build a pedestrian-oriented road that could improve the road line and prevent traffic safety accidents.
