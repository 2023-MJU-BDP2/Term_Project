▲ 여수해양경찰서가 가을철 낚시어선과 레저기구 이용객 증가에 따라 해상 음주 운항 특별 단속을 벌이기로 했다.







여수해양경찰서는 6일 가을철 낚시어선과 레저기구 이용객 증가에 따라 해상 음주 운항 특별 단속에 나선다고 밝혔다.

여수해경에 따르면 오는 10일까지 계도기간을 거쳐 11일부터 30일까지 해상 음주 운항 특별단속을 실시하기로 했다.

특별단속은 가을철 갑오징어, 갈치 등 낚시 최성수기에 낚시어선과 레저기구 등 음주 운항으로 인한 사고를 예방하기 위해 해상교통관제센터(VTS), 경비함정, 파출소 등 해·육상 연계 합동단속으로 진행된다.

단속 대상은 다중이용선박(유람선·도선·낚시어선), 어선, 수상레저기구 등 모든선박으로 특히 ▲낚시어선 주 조업지 ▲레저기구, 소형선박 등의 주요 활동지 ▲활동 시기를 고려해 단속을펼친다.

여수해경 관계자는 "음주 운항은 해양 사고로 이어져 타인의 생명, 신체, 재산상의 피해를 일으키며 해양오염 사고로도 이어질 수 있다"며 "안전한 해양문화 정착을 위해 해양 종사자나 낚시·레저기구 운항자분들의 각별한 주의가 필요하다"고 말했다.

Yeosu Coast Guard, special crackdown on drunken driving on fishing boats and water leisure equipment...For all ships from the 11th to the 30th of this month reporter goYongbae

Yeosu Maritime Police Station announced on the 6th that it is launching a special crackdown on drunken driving at sea due to the increase in the number of fishing boats and leisure equipment users in the fall season.

According to the Yeosu Coast Guard, it was decided to conduct a special crackdown on drunken driving at sea from the 11th to the 30th, following an guidance period until the 10th.

The special crackdown is carried out through a joint crackdown in conjunction with the sea and land, including the Maritime Traffic Control Center (VTS), guard ships, and police stations, to prevent accidents caused by drunken operation of fishing boats and leisure equipment during the peak fishing season for cuttlefish and cutlassfish in the fall.

The target of the crackdown is all vessels, including multi-use vessels (cruising boats, pilot boats, fishing boats), fishing boats, and water leisure equipment. In particular, crackdowns will be carried out taking into account ▲ the main operating area of fishing boats, ▲ the main activity areas of leisure equipment, small vessels, etc. ▲ and the period of activity.

An official from the Yeosu Coast Guard said, “Drunk sailing can lead to marine accidents, causing damage to the life, body, and property of others, and can also lead to marine pollution accidents.” He added, “To establish a safe maritime culture, marine workers and operators of fishing and leisure equipment should “Special caution is needed,” he said.
