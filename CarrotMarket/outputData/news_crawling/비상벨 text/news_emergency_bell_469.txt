Incheon City strengthens customized support for each life cycle.

Overcoming low birth rates, expanding parental benefits, supporting pregnancy and infertility, and alleviating the burden of medical expenses, etc.

-Break News Incheon Reporter Park Sang-do

On the 9th, Incheon City announced that it would strengthen its care system more closely from infants to the elderly.

According to Incheon City, the city requests a budget of 3.0215 trillion won in the field of women and family in 2024 and significantly strengthens customized support by age and type, such as overcoming low birth rates, supporting the growth of the weak, and supporting social activities of the elderly.

Next year's budget request is an increase of 341 billion won, or about 12.7%, from this year's 2.6805 trillion won.

○ To ensure happiness in childbirth and childcare...significantly increase parental benefits and eliminate restrictions on medical expenses for high-risk pregnant women.

Incheon City prepares for the future based on ‘Incheon where childbirth and childcare are happy choices.’

First, parental benefits will be greatly expanded. This year, 700,000 won for 0-year-old children and 350,000 won for 1-year-old children will be provided, but next year, it will increase to 1 million won for 0-year-old children and 500,000 won for 1-year-old children, and the number of beneficiaries is expected to be about 17,000. .

The burden of medical expenses to support pregnancy and infertility will also be greatly reduced.

Incheon City plans to support medical expenses for high-risk pregnant women, which was previously only provided to households with 180% or less of the median income, regardless of income, and the number of beneficiaries is expected to increase by about 1,000 (approximately 1,600) compared to the previous year.

Additionally, when using frozen eggs before infertility diagnosis, the cost of assisted reproductive technology will be supported up to 1 million won per session for up to 33 people.

In particular, to ensure safe transportation of infants and toddlers to daycare centers, 4,000 new 3-point seat belts will be provided to 605 daycare vehicles in the district.

In addition, as Incheon is a city of openness and inclusiveness with people from all over the world, childcare services for children of ethnic Koreans living abroad have been expanded this year from 1 to 4 years old to 0-year-olds, and childcare subsidies, which were previously supported only by non-profit private organizations, have been expanded to include daycare centers, allowing for a greater choice of childcare facilities. widened

○ Increase the meal price for undernourished children to 9,000 won and strengthen the infrastructure to prevent and support children who are victims of abuse.

The unit meal price for children at risk of missing meals will be increased from 8,000 won to 9,000 won this year.

This is to alleviate children's nutritional imbalance as the average cost of eating out for major items has recently exceeded 8,000 won due to rising prices.

Currently, there are about 13,000 people eligible for meal support in Incheon City, and it is expected that menu options for children receiving support will be expanded.

The scope of Didim Seed Accounts will be greatly expanded so that children from low-income families can establish a solid foundation for independence when they become adults.

Currently, only children aged 12 to 17 from households receiving livelihood and medical benefits can sign up for a bank account, but from 2024, it will be expanded to include housing and education benefits, and the age range will also be expanded from 0 to 17 years old.

The Didim Seed Account is an account in which if a child saves within the range of 50,000 won per month, Incheon City accumulates twice the amount saved, up to a maximum of 100,000 won, including government funds.

Support for educational activities for children of multicultural families is being promoted as a new project.

For school-age children from multicultural families whose median income is between 50% and 100% of the median income, we provide annual support of 400,000 won for elementary school students, 500,000 won for middle school students, and 600,000 won for high school students to cover the costs of educational activities such as purchasing textbooks, using the reading room, and purchasing online learning tickets.

Above all, we will strengthen infrastructure to prevent child abuse and support child victims.

We plan to expand one additional child protection agency to support children who are victims of child abuse, increasing the total to six.

The number of child abuse cases per child protection agency in Incheon City is 443, which is higher than the national average of 329 cases. As the number of child protection agencies increases, the number of abuse case management decreases, leading to professional and comprehensive in-depth case management. It is expected that protection of children affected will be strengthened.

In addition, the ‘Incheon Child Love Education Center’, the nation’s first permanent education center for child abuse prevention, will also be opened to raise awareness of abuse prevention through parent education.

○ Promote new job training for out-of-school youth and housing support for stalking victims

In order to strengthen the independence and employment support of out-of-school youth, customized vocational training (up to 3.6 million won per person) and internship/work experience (up to 2 million won per person) will be established at the Incheon City Out-of-School Youth Support Center, and employment linkage will also be supported.

As a new project for women and victims of domestic violence, 5 houses have been secured to support emergency housing for stalking victims, and there are currently two Women's Safety Dream projects that support the installation of safe home sets and emergency bells for single-person female households and stores. Expands from 1 district to 6 districts.

In addition, we plan to strengthen the prevention of domestic violence through the operation of a public-private-government working-level consultative body, linkage with family counseling, and on-site counseling.

○ Incheon, the best city for jobs for the elderly... Largest jobs ever, 100 smart senior centers

The elderly job support project will be expanded to the largest scale ever.

In 2024, we plan to support 54,123 jobs for the elderly, which is an increase of 7,477 from this year. In addition to discovering new jobs such as senior video filming and editing crews and reading assistants, public interest jobs are worth 290,000 won and social service jobs are worth 634,000 won. The activity fee will also be increased by 20,000 won and 40,000 won, respectively.

In addition, the senior center, which represents local senior leisure welfare facilities, plans to build 100 smart senior centers to expand health and leisure welfare services, and to provide digital services such as virtual reality fitness devices so that seniors can have a healthy and enjoyable retirement in the age of super-aging. Create a leisure space for the elderly using devices.
